8: 1. Overview
9: 1.1 Purpose
10: AlgoConnect is an internal B2B sales-enablement platform for identifying, verifying, and marketing our algorithmic trading products to SEBI/NSE-authorised market intermediaries — Research Analysts (RAs), Investment Advisers (IAs), and sub-brokers — who are legally positioned to distribute algo strategies to end clients. The platform converts publicly available regulatory registers into a qualified, enriched, and actively-managed outbound pipeline across Email, WhatsApp, and SMS.
11: 1.2 Problem Statement
12: We hold large static lead lists (RA / IA / sub-broker registers published by SEBI and NSE) but no systematic way to tell which entities are still active, what they currently sell, or how to reach them.
13: Outreach today is manual and channel-fragmented; there is no unified view of a lead across Email, WhatsApp, and SMS, and no consent/compliance trail.
14: We need a repeatable pipeline: ingest → verify/enrich → segment → engage → track → hand off to sales, that can scale from hundreds to tens of thousands of leads.
15: 1.3 Scope
16: Phase 1 (this spec): India equity/derivatives algo product distribution, targeting NSE-listed algo offerings sold through SEBI-registered RAs, IAs, and NSE-authorised persons/sub-brokers.
17: Phase 2 (future, not built now but data model must accommodate): Crypto market algo products, currently unregulated in India — the data model and campaign engine are designed market-agnostic from day one so Crypto can be enabled as a second "market" without re-architecture.

19: 3. Core Workflow
20: The product is built around a five-stage pipeline that mirrors the process described by the business:
21: Ingest — import raw lead lists sourced from SEBI/NSE public registers (RA, IA, sub-broker/authorised-person lists).
22: Verify & Enrich — determine whether each entity is currently active, and collect supplementary operating detail (website, socials, apparent client focus, AUM/scale signals, existing product stack) via web lookup.
23: Segment & Score — group and rank leads by activity confidence, fit, and reachability.
24: Engage — run multi-channel campaigns (Email / WhatsApp / SMS) with consent and throttling controls.
25: Track & Hand off — capture engagement events (opens, clicks, replies, opt-outs) and push qualified leads to the sales/CRM queue.
27: 4. Feature List by Module
28: 4.1 Lead Import & Management
29: Bulk import via CSV/XLSX upload, with column-mapping UI (name, entity type, registration no., address, phone, email, region).
30: De-duplication on registration number + normalised phone/email.
31: Source tracking: every lead stores its origin register (e.g. "SEBI RA List — June 2026") and import batch ID.
32: Manual lead entry and edit for one-off additions.
33: Market tag on every lead: India-Equity (Phase 1) or Crypto (Phase 2, disabled by default).
34: 4.2 Enrichment Engine
35: Automated, queued enrichment job per lead:
36: Activity check: search the web for the entity/registration number to flag Active, Likely Inactive, or Unverified, with the evidence links stored against the lead.
37: Firm profile capture: website URL, social handles, apparent focus area (equity/derivatives/algo/advisory), estimated scale signals (employee count, client testimonials, years active).
38: Existing tech-stack signal: whether the lead already advertises or resells a competing algo/trading product.
39: Confidence score (0–100) per enrichment field, and a re-verification cadence (e.g. re-check every 90 days) so stale "active" flags don't linger.
40: Enrichment is provider-agnostic behind an internal interface — implementation may call a search API or an LLM-assisted lookup service; only public information is collected, never data behind logins or paywalls.
41: 4.3 Segmentation & Scoring
42: Rule-based and saved-filter segments (e.g. "Active RAs, Maharashtra, no existing algo product").
43: Lead score combining: activity confidence, registration type weight, enrichment completeness, and prior engagement (if any).
44: Segment size preview before a campaign is launched.
45: 4.4 Campaign & Messaging
46: Channel-specific campaign builder for Email, WhatsApp, and SMS, each with its own template library.
47: Approved-template enforcement: WhatsApp and SMS sends are blocked unless the template is pre-approved (see Section 6.2).
48: Sequencing: multi-step drip sequences with wait steps and branch-on-engagement logic (e.g. "if opened but no click, send WhatsApp follow-up on day 3").
49: Send-time throttling per channel to respect provider rate limits and avoid spam flags.
50: Per-lead channel preference and unified do-not-contact flag that suppresses all channels at once.
51: 4.5 Compliance & Consent Management
52: Consent ledger per lead per channel (opted-in, opted-out, unknown/implied-B2B), with timestamp and source of the consent event.
53: One-click / auto-processed opt-out handling (STOP replies, WhatsApp block, unsubscribe link) that propagates instantly to all future campaigns.
54: National DND / NCPR suppression check before any SMS/voice send.
55: Immutable audit log of every message sent, to whom, under which template, and consent basis — exportable for a compliance review.
56: 4.6 Analytics & Reporting
57: Funnel view: imported → enriched → active-confirmed → contacted → engaged → handed-off-to-sales.
58: Per-campaign delivery, open, click, reply, and opt-out rates by channel.
59: Enrichment coverage and data-freshness dashboard (% of leads verified in last 90 days).
60: 4.7 Admin & Access Control
61: Role-based access (Growth Operator, Compliance Admin, Sales Rep, System Admin).
62: API key / credential vault for Email, WhatsApp, and SMS provider integrations.
63: Full activity audit trail for admin actions.
64: 4.8 Future: Crypto Market Module (Phase 2, design-only for now)
65: No India crypto algo-distribution rule currently exists, so this module ships disabled. The data model (Section 5) already carries a market field on Lead and Campaign so this can be turned on later without a schema migration. When enabled it will need its own lead source (crypto exchanges/VASPs, influencer/community lists) and its own compliance ruleset, to be scoped separately once the regulatory picture is clearer.
67: 5. Data Model
68: Core entities and key fields. Primary/foreign keys and standard audit columns (created_at, updated_at, created_by) are implied on every entity and omitted below for brevity.
69: Lead
70: A single RA / IA / sub-broker / authorised person record.
71: ImportBatch
72: One bulk-upload event of a source register.
73: EnrichmentRecord
74: One enrichment pass on a Lead; a lead can have several over time.
75: Consent
76: Per-lead, per-channel consent/opt-out state.
77: Campaign
78: An outbound messaging campaign targeting one or more segments.
79: MessageTemplate
80: A reusable, compliance-approved message per channel.
81: MessageSend
82: One individual message dispatched to one lead.
83: EngagementEvent
84: A tracked interaction with a sent message.
85: User
86: Internal platform user.
88: 6. API & Integration Requirements
89: 6.1 Inbound / Data Sourcing
90: No official SEBI/NSE bulk-data API exists for these registers; ingestion is via manually downloaded CSV/XLSX from the public SEBI/NSE intermediary lists, through the standard Lead Import module.
91: Enrichment lookups use a general web-search/fetch provider behind an internal EnrichmentProvider interface, so the underlying vendor can be swapped without touching calling code.
92: 6.2 Outbound Channel APIs
93: All three integrations should sit behind a single internal MessagingGateway interface exposing send(), getStatus(), and a webhook receiver for delivery/opt-out callbacks — this keeps Campaign/MessageSend logic provider-agnostic.
94: 6.3 Internal APIs (indicative)
95: POST /leads/import — accepts a mapped CSV/XLSX, creates an ImportBatch and Lead rows.
96: POST /leads/{id}/enrich — queues an enrichment job; GET /leads/{id}/enrichment for history.
97: GET /segments/preview — accepts a filter payload, returns matching lead count.
98: POST /campaigns — create/update a campaign; POST /campaigns/{id}/launch.
99: POST /webhooks/email|whatsapp|sms — provider delivery/engagement callbacks, written into MessageSend / EngagementEvent.
100: POST /consent/opt-out — manual or automated opt-out entry point, propagates to Consent and do_not_contact.
101: 7. Non-Functional Requirements
102: 7.1 Data Protection & Privacy
103: Lead contact data (phone, email) is personal data under India's Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act — store only what's needed, encrypt at rest, and log every access.
104: Provide a data-retention policy and a mechanism to purge a lead's data on request.
105: 7.2 Compliance Guardrails
106: A message cannot be sent on any channel if do_not_contact is true or the channel-level Consent status is OPTED_OUT — enforced at the MessagingGateway layer, not just the UI.
107: SMS sends must fail closed (not send) if DLT template registration or NCPR scrub status is missing or stale.
108: 7.3 Scalability & Reliability
109: Enrichment and messaging are queue-based (async workers) so a bulk import of tens of thousands of leads doesn't block the UI.
110: Provider webhooks are idempotent and retried on failure.
111: 7.4 Auditability
112: Every send, consent change, and admin action is logged with actor, timestamp, and before/after state for compliance review.
